<h3>Checking Strings</h3>
<p>Sometimes we have to check whether or not a certain character, word, or phrase is present in the string. To do so, we can use the in and not in operators.</p>

<br>
<h5>Example</h5>
<example>
print("hello" in "Hello World!")
print("hello" not in "Hello World!")
print("World" in "Hello World!")
print("World" not in "Hello World!")
</example>

<br>
<h5>Output</h5>
<example>
False
True
True
False
</example>

<br>
<h3>String Concatenation</h3>
<p>Using the plus sign (+), we can concatenate or combine two strings into one.</p>

<br>
<h5>Example</h5>
<example>
a = "Python"
b = "3"

c = a + b
print(c)

print(a + " " + b)
</example>

<br>
<h5>Output</h5>
<example>
Python3
Python 3
</example>
